Into d'Groove — EP Review
9/10

Too Tall To See The Moon

Freak Pointing

Having never yet heard of the band, I wasn't sure what I was about to expose my ears to, but all I can say is that halfway through the first track I was already hooked, by midway of Third, that was it, I am a fan!

The Circle Beneath

Opens with some heavy baselines bringing back some 80's Goth vibes, but no, wait, hang on, what's that pick scrape creating a contrasting punk element, switching out for '76 guitar, followed by vox in true punk style, noise increases rhythmic, anarchic, melodic, a track to get you bouncing & singing along, with a nice guitar solo midway, a sound that this Gen X journalist was very happy to hear, equally catering to 14yo modern day punksters.

Third

Instantly brought my mind to another track with my exclaiming in delight “Oooo” — Rezillos/Revillos meets Freak Pointing! This EP is hitting every chord of memories galore, cranking it out in their own way, its fresh, its loud, its crisp in its delivery. I cannot wait to see these guys live!

Whoa, the way the build ups switch sounds yet keep with the themes is quite something, Eton Rifles blast from the past flew off the bass & guitar in a new song all of its own. Now… how the heck are these guys doing this? Violent Femmes enter the mix, not to negate the sheer musical prowess of this band, they have cleverly woven nuances of every punk era into their songs! Lyrics resonating with the populace!

Thumb Tacks

Drums lead the way marching us into baselines that are all so delectable, next that 60's guitar riff, followed with that voice that speaks so loud & raw, gritty, authentic. This track brings a rockier element to it, maturing out of classic punk into something deeper & more hypnotic with that guitar solo.

Unwound

Grunge opener crashes in, slowing to bass/drum combo before vocals layer the track into a sound blast. The lyrics hit serious subject matter in its melodic formation.

“Well what a delight this EP is, highly recommended. These guys have delivered not only a listenable release, but a highly enjoyable one.”
Dorn Simon Into d'Groove @intodgroove
